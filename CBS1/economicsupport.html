Economic support

Socio-group members do not get any financial incentive or sitting allowance for participating in the group sessions. The rationale for this is that it would bypass the goal of sociotherapy, which relates to breaking the circle of victimhood and dependency, to empower people and restore their dignity, to create connectedness and group support and encourage people to remain motivated by the purpose of the group sessions and not by financial motivations. Participation through intrinsic motivation is one of the core pillars of the CBS philosophy. In addition to that, not providing sitting allowances will contribute to sustainability of the approach and ownership.
However, in all CBS projects, it has been observed that after the completion of 15 sessions, many sociotherapy groups decide to continue meeting. Not only to sustain their group cohesion, but also to initiate income generating activities together for their personal, family and community socio-economic wellbeing. Some groups start small livelihood projects and other groups saving and credit groups (e.g. Voluntary Saving and Loan Associations (VSLA)).
CBS Rwanda therefore supports graduate sociotherapy groups with an action plan and demonstrating a need of support in terms of capacity building regarding their socio-economic initiatives through coaching and training, seed funding and/or linking to organizations that may be of support of their income generating activities.
