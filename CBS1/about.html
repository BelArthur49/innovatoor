BIO

Emmanuel Sarabwe

Head of Programs in CBs Rwanda

Bio

Emmanuel Sarabwe has an MA degree of Social Work and Social administration from the Uganda Christian University in Kabale, Uganda, obtained in 2012 and a second MA degree of Human Rights Gender and Conflict in Social Justice Perspective from the International Institute of Social Studies in the Hague, The Netherlands, obtained in 2017.

Since 2005, he has been working for successive programs of Community Based Sociotherapy, supporting Rwandans to constructively deal with the consequences of the genocide against the Tutsi. He conducted applied research in the areas of family dynamism, psychosocial life, forgiveness and peacebuilding. Currently, he is conducting research on
1) Forgiveness as reparative humanism of genocide crimes in Rwanda and
2) In the underarm of God: Surviving from community resistance to the genocide against Tutsi in Rwanda.

BIO

Reverien Interayamahanga

Social researcher

Bio

Interayamahanga Reverien is a Rwandan social researcher specializing in peacebuilding, mental health, gender and participatory governance studies.

He holds a Master of Arts in Development Sociology from Wits University (South Africa) and a Master of Science in Gender and Development from the University of Rwanda.

He is currently a Senior Researcher with Community-Based Sociotherapy (CBS) Rwanda, where he co- leads and supports studies on community healing, social cohesion, and resilience in post- genocide Rwanda , in the framework of sociotherapy .

Prior to joining CBS, he worked successively with the Human Rights League in the Great Lakes Region (LDGL), Kigali Independent University, the Institute of Research and Dialogue for Peace (IRDP), and Never Again Rwanda, contributing to research, training, and community-based peacebuilding initiatives.

He has also taught Political Sociology and Research Methods for the Social Sciences at local universities, and has led or co-led numerous baseline surveys, evaluations, and qualitative studies with several organizations and institutions both local and international. His research and context analyses extend beyond Rwanda to cover peace and conflict dynamics, human rights, and governance trends in the Great Lakes Region.

His work bridges the fields of reconciliation, resilience, gender, mental health and psychosocial support (MHPSS), participatory governance , environmental governance, advocacy combining strong field coordination and analytical skills with a deep commitment to evidence-based policy and programming..

Psychologist

Emmanuel Nzabonimpa

Title

Bio

Emmanuel Nzabonimpa is a public health professional with bachelor in community health from Uganda Christian University, and master’s in public health with specialization in Monitoring and Evaluation from Mount Kenya University.

He also holds a Diploma in health services management from Kabale University and an accredited Diploma in Groupwork practice from Institute of Group Analysis (UK).

Before joining CBS Rwanda, he served as district hospital supervisor in charge of Health information management system, Maternal and Child health, Malaria and HIV prevention and a treatment as well as Project officer in a non- government organization. Since 2014, Emmanuel is working with CBS Rwanda, he served as Project district Officer, a Participatory Action researcher and Project Coordinator.

He contributes to the capacity building of CBS group facilitators through offering trainings on CBS approach and field supportive supervision and meetings.

He a member of CBS research team and participated in the design and implementation of different, qualitative and quantitative studies aiming at assessing effectiveness of CBS approach in different contexts particularly supporting in study design, data collection and analysis. In terms of publication He authored a mixed method study on social isolation among ex-genocide prisoners in Rwanda and co-authored a c-RCT protocol on evaluating the impact of Community-Based Sociotherapy on social dignity in post-genocide Rwanda.
